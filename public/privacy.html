1. Who we are

TeacherAI (teacherai.ca) is an AI-assisted lesson planning, assessment, and reporting tool built for Ontario K–8 teachers, including those with split-grade classrooms. TeacherAI is built and operated by Ontario teachers and is not affiliated with any school board.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use TeacherAI.

3. Student data

⚠️
MFIPPA notice: Student data collected through TeacherAI may be subject to the Municipal Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (MFIPPA) in Ontario. We recommend using first names only, initials, or pseudonyms whenever possible. Do not upload full IEP documents — paraphrase accommodations in your own professional notes.

TeacherAI collects minimal student information — first names, grade level, and teacher-written accommodation notes. This is limited to what a teacher would write in a lesson plan or class list.

What we do not collect:

  • Student email addresses or passwords (students have no accounts)
  • Student photos or biometric data
  • Psychological assessments or full IEP documents
  • Parent/guardian contact information
  • Student Ontario Education Numbers (OEN)

Students access worksheets through a shared link — they type their first name to identify themselves. No student account is created.

4. How AI is used

TeacherAI uses the Anthropic Claude API to generate lesson plans, worksheets, rubrics, and report card comments. When content is generated:

  • Student names are never sent to Anthropic. Students are anonymized as "Student A, Student B" etc. before any AI call.
  • Learning profiles are anonymized. Modified expectations are sent with anonymized labels only.
  • Assessment data is never sent to Anthropic. Marks and grades stay on Canadian servers only.
  • Worksheet submissions are never sent to Anthropic. Student answers stay in our database only.

What IS sent to Anthropic: topic, grade level, subject, duration, anonymized learning needs, and class context notes. Anthropic's API does not use API data to train models by default. For details, see Anthropic's Privacy Policy.

5. Data storage & residency

All TeacherAI data is stored in Canada on AWS infrastructure in Montreal, Quebec (ca-central-1 region), accessed through Supabase, our database and authentication provider.

This means your data — including student names, assessment records, lesson content, and worksheet submissions — never leaves Canada at rest. Canadian data residency supports compliance with PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act, federal) and MFIPPA (Municipal Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act, Ontario) requirements for Ontario educators.

Data in transit is encrypted via HTTPS/TLS. Data at rest is encrypted by Supabase's storage layer. Database access is restricted by row-level security policies that scope every record to the teacher who created it.

Security incident reporting: If we discover a privacy breach affecting your data, we will notify affected users within 72 hours of discovery and report to the Privacy Commissioner of Canada when required under PIPEDA's Mandatory Breach of Security Safeguards regulations.

7. Retention & deletion

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. Specifically:

  • Lessons and worksheets: Retained indefinitely until you delete them or your account
  • Worksheet submissions: Retained until you delete them or your account
  • Assessment data: Retained until you delete sessions or your account
  • Student roster: Retained until you remove students or your account

You can delete individual students, lessons, and assessment sessions directly from the app. You can also request full account deletion from the in-app account menu, which removes all associated data within 30 days.

Worksheet submissions older than one school year may be automatically purged at the start of the next school year. We recommend exporting any submissions you wish to retain long-term.

8. Your rights

Under PIPEDA (the federal Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) and applicable Ontario privacy law, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of all data we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and all associated data
  • Portability: Export your data (CSV export available in the Grade Tracker)
  • Withdraw consent: Stop using TeacherAI at any time and request deletion
  • Complain: File a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada if you believe we've mishandled your data

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email address below. We will acknowledge your request within 5 business days and respond fully within 30 days.

9. MFIPPA & Ontario schools

TeacherAI is designed with Ontario's privacy landscape in mind, including the Municipal Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (MFIPPA), which governs student data in Ontario school boards.

Recommendations for Ontario teachers:

  • Use first names only, initials, or pseudonyms in the class roster whenever possible
  • Do not paste full IEP documents — use your own professional notes summarizing modified expectations only
  • Check your board's acceptable use policy before entering student data into any third-party tool
  • If your board requires a Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) before approving tools, contact us — we can provide documentation to support that process

TeacherAI is not currently approved by any specific Ontario school board. Individual teachers use this tool under their own professional judgment. We are actively working toward formal board approval and Privacy Impact Assessment processes — contact us if your board would like to evaluate TeacherAI.
